Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about logging drives, exports, and how Permit2Drive works.

Getting Started

What is Permit2Drive?

Permit2Drive is a simple app for tracking supervised driving hours. It helps you log drives quickly, track totals, and export clean records when needed.

Who is Permit2Drive for?

Permit2Drive is primarily designed for learner drivers who need to track supervised driving hours as they work toward getting their license.

It can also be useful for families who want a simple shared driving log, for driver training situations with multiple supervisors, or for anyone who wants an easy way to keep a detailed personal driving record.

Do I need to create an account?

No new Permit2Drive account is required. You can use the app in Local Only mode with no sign-in at all.

If you want backup, syncing, or shared access, you can connect your Google account. Your data is stored in your own Google Drive —not in a separate Permit2Drive account—and you stay in control.

That means no new passwords to remember and no extra email spam from us.

What is the difference between Local Only and Google logs?

Permit2Drive gives you two ways to save your driving log: Google Drive or Local Only .

Google Drive is the recommended option for most families. Your log is saved in your own Google Drive, which makes it easier to back up your data, share access with another user, and continue using the same log if you change devices.

Google logs also allow multiple users to contribute to the same driving log. This is helpful when a learner driver has more than one parent, guardian, instructor, or supervisor helping with supervised driving.

Local Only keeps your driving log on your device and does not require signing in. However, Local Only logs stay on that device and cannot be shared with other users . If you use Local Only, we recommend exporting a CSV backup occasionally so you have a copy outside your phone.

You stay in control either way. Permit2Drive does not create a separate account for you, and Google logs remain stored in your own Google Drive.

Can I switch from Local Only to Google Drive later?

Yes. You can start with Local Only and later connect Google Drive if you want backup, sharing, or access from another device.

If you start in Local Only mode, remember that the log stays on that device until you connect or export your data. Local Only logs cannot be shared with other users.

Which log option should I choose?

For most families, we recommend using Google Drive. It gives you backup, sharing, and an easier path if you change devices.

Local Only is best if you want the simplest setup, do not need sharing, and are comfortable exporting your own backups.

Where is my data stored?

If you use Google sync, your driving log is stored in your own Google Drive .

If you use Local Only mode, your data stays on your device. It is a good idea to export a backup occasionally. If your phone is lost, reset, or reinstalled, local-only data may be lost unless you have saved a backup.

Can I change how the app looks?

Yes. Permit2Drive includes appearance options so you can choose a visual style that fits your preference.

Changing the app appearance does not affect your driving data, saved logs, exports, or sharing settings.

Logging Drives

What can I track in a drive?

You can track drive time, day or night driving, weather, road type, and your own notes. This makes it easier to keep a detailed record and review specific driving conditions later.

Do you support multiple drivers?

Yes. Permit2Drive is especially helpful for families with more than one learner driver.

Do you support multiple supervisors?

Yes. You can track drives with different supervisors, which is helpful when a learner drives with more than one parent, guardian, instructor, or other approved adult.

Can others contribute to my drive log?

Yes—if you use a Google Drive log. Sharing is not available for Local Only logs because those logs stay on one device.

Create your driving log, then open the menu and select Share Drive Log . That person can then add drives to the log.

You stay in control of who has access, and the log remains stored in your Google Drive .

Can I edit a drive after saving?

Yes. You can update or correct drives at any time before exporting.

Can I pause and resume a drive?

Yes. You can pause a drive and resume it later—useful for errands or breaks.

Importing & Backup

How do I import from RoadReady?

In the app menu, select Import , then choose your RoadReady file. Permit2Drive will bring in your existing drives so you can continue without starting over.

Should I back up my drives if I use Local Only?

Yes. If you use Local Only mode, it is a good idea to export a CSV backup occasionally. This gives you a copy outside your phone in case something happens to your device.

How do I back up my drives if I use Local Only?

In the app menu, select Export , then choose the CSV option.

Save the file somewhere secure outside your phone, such as cloud storage or a computer.

You can later import that file back into Permit2Drive if your phone is lost, replaced, or reset.

Purchases & Family Sharing

How do I restore my Ad-Free purchase?

If you already purchased Ad-Free and later reinstalled Permit2Drive, changed devices, or do not see Ad-Free active, open the app menu and use Restore Purchases .

Restore Purchases works with the same Apple App Store or Google Play account that was used to make the original purchase.

If the purchase was made with a different store account, you may need to switch to that account first and then try restoring again.

Can I share Ad-Free with my family?

On Apple devices, Ad-Free may be eligible for Apple Family Sharing if Family Sharing is enabled for the purchase and your Apple account settings allow shared purchases.

Apple notes that family members may need to use the app’s restore flow to access shared non-consumable in-app purchases. In Permit2Drive, open the app menu and use Restore Purchases on the family member’s device.

Family Sharing availability depends on Apple account settings, purchase sharing settings, and App Store eligibility. Google Play family purchase behavior may differ.

Privacy & Data

Do you collect my data?

Permit2Drive is designed to minimize data collection. When using Google sync, your data is stored in your own Google Drive—not on our servers.

Do you show ads?

Yes, some versions of Permit2Drive may show ads. Ads help support the app and keep it available to more families.

If you prefer an ad-free experience, Permit2Drive offers a one-time Ad-Free purchase that removes ads from the app.

The Ad-Free purchase is handled through the Apple App Store or Google Play, depending on your device and does not affect your driving data, your saved logs, or your exported records.
